Human Trafficking Defined

Human Trafficking- a crime of exploitation of someone for the purposes of compelled labor or a commercial sex act.

There are two types of Trafficking-

  • Sex Trafficking: modern-day slavery where individuals perform commercial sex through the use of force, fraud, or coercion.
  • Labor Trafficking: modern-day slavery where individuals perform labor or services through the use of force, fraud, or coercion.

In the state of Utah, the law clearly defines a human trafficking victim as any minor under the age of 18 used in a commercial sex act without regard to use of force, fraud, or coercion, aligning to the federal trafficking law. This means that any minor used in a commercial sex act is a human trafficking victim.

Human Trafficking Statistics

  • Human Trafficking is a multi-billion dollar criminal industry that denies freedom to 24.9 million people around the world.
  • In 2020, there were 10,583 reported cases to the National Human Trafficking Hotline
  • Of these cases, 4,691 High Indicator victims and 12,325 Moderate Indicator victims.
  • Of the 10,583 cases, about 80% of victims were female, 12% were male, and the rest were gender minorities or unknown
  • Again, of these 10,583 cases, 2,488 of these were minors and the rest were adults
  • Sex Trafficking accounted for 7,648 of these cases
  • Labor Trafficking accounted for 1,052 of these cases

These statistics are from the National Human Trafficking Hotline website. To learn more, visit their website here
